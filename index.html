The Chair




The chair is a single loop in space, demarcating the boundary of the seated individual and intertwining interior and exterior space. The seat--left void by the frame--is woven into place, making visible the hand of the maker.

The Motel that Goes to Sea




The motel draws on the scalar continuity between the highway lane and the bedroom, and consequently, the car and the bed. Stitching together the road, the land, and the sea, the motel is reminiscent of the aqueduct-like Overseas Highway that traverses the Gulf of Mexico to connect the entire length of the Florida Keys. The horizontal positioning of the motel directly over the highway emphasizes the tension and incongruity of the artifi cial and the natural on the site. The motel responds by suggesting an uncanny relationship between environment and program; the pool is found inside the ocean, the beach within the median, and parking beneath the bed. What remains consistent is the passage through the site by the building’s arches, which grow to accommodate different programmatic needs, but retain a uniform relationship of road to car to room to bed. The motel also relates to the Overseas Highway in name, as the route had been nicknamed in the 1930s the “highway that goes to sea.”

The Gatehouse




Given a site on the Gowanus Canal in Brooklyn, this project focused on designing a gatehouse on the canal. The gatekeeper is the Ancient Mariner from Samuel Taylor Coleridge’s “The Rime of the Ancient Mariner"; the poem is split into six parts, and each becomes a sectional moment that recreates the psychological or emotional state of the episode through architecture.

Gallery for William Kentridge




A system of screens creates an immersive stage for William Kentridge's performative art works, playing with perspective so as to lead the viewer through the entire act. These screens shift and recombine to form not just the two-dimensional "stage," but also circulation and seating. The building draws from the conditions of the site to bring even the passerby into the space of performance.

Performance Space for Falconworks




A performance and teaching space for Falconworks, an avant-garde theater group in Brooklyn, on an industrial grain terminal site in Red Hook. The figural lines imply abstract spaces of performance throughout the entire site and delineate interior space within the building volumes.

Bas Relief




Like a traditional carved bas relief, this piece is only three inches deep, but through the use of perspective and illusion implies an immense space beyond its narrow frame.

The Playbook




The playbook is composed of every page in the Oxford English Dictionary that contains the word "performance." The pages are excised and linearly notated to trace the progression of "performance" throughout the text. The book itself is a performative object, while taking on the spatial and structural attributes of the media.

Quarry Dwelling




Located in Stony Creek Quarry in Branford, CT, this one-man dwelling allows the visitor to encounter the vastness of the quarry from intimate nooks and windows, creating platforms for rest and viewing.

The Dominant Void




A sculpture that aims to express a void more palpably than its own material. Made of 1x2 wood sticks, the piece was designed to be viewed in the service stairwell of Paul Rudolph's Yale School of Architecture.

The Analytic Model, Villa Shodhan




A semester-long study of Le Corbusier’s Villa Shodhan (1951-56) as an analysis of the formal and conceptual armature of the building. The four models and drawings address the building's tectonics, contour, massing, and program.

Villa in the Landscape




Focusing on framed views, each unit of the villa is made up of massive chamfered walls from which programmatic elements are carved. Though each unit functions as a frame for the next, the chamfering of the walls renders the view beyond seemingly two-dimensional, challenging the significance of the successive view.

Kit of Parts




The villa Casa Malaparte (1937) is reinterpreted as a toy-like set of core principles -- a "kit of parts." The kit, when closed, operates as a prison-like cell, but opens to become an infinitely variable landscape into which stairs must lock to hold it in place. The villa’s monumental staircase thus becomes the essential tectonic element of the entire landscape.

Senior Research Colloquium: "Spatial Encounters in Japan"




A detailed analysis of the gassho-zukuri farmhouse as a way of demonstrating the metaphysical tectonic framework of Japanese architecture that gives form to dwelling. I suggest in my Senior Essay that the intimate spatial encounters of the built environment all ensue from the demarcation of boundaries. Through these tectonic bindings, scale becomes fluidly interconnected, condensing into a single manifestation of space and dwelling. Tectonics in the gassho-zukuri house – and in much of Japanese architecture – may therefore be understood as generating a sense of occupation, dwelling, and place, at once constructing intimate interiority while also connecting space with the vastness of the landscape.

Japan, Robert C. Bates Fellowship and Summer Fellowship in Japan




This six-week independent summer research project focused on spatial concepts of Japanese carpentry, specifically wooden joinery, and its relation to aesthetic philosophy. The project served as a foundation for a senior project in the architecture major.

Arizona, Harvey Geiger Fellowship in Architecture




With the Harvey Geiger Fellowship, I pursued an independent research project into the American pilgrimage to the National Parks, visiting the Grand Canyon and the Antelope Canyon in Arizona. The project focused on the idea of an aesthetic pilgrimage and its relation to the vastness of these natural geologies.

"Rethinking the Lightbulb," Oct. 2015 Exhibit in the JE Gallery




The goal of the Lightbulb Initiative, started in 2014 by the Office of Sustainability, was to reduce energy consumption by offering students more efficient LEDs in exchange for their incandescent lightbulbs. Using these recycled incandescent bulbs, “Rethinking the Lightbulb” is an art installation that takes on the challenge of illuminating the gallery space using less energy, complementing the effort to minimize the use of new material.
